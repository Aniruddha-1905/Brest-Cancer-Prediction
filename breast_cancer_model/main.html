Breast Cancer Information

Define cancer :
Cancer is a disease characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells in the body.

Define breast cancer:
Breast cancer is a malignant tumor that develops in the breast tissue, often starting in the milk ducts or lobules and potentially spreading to other parts of the body.


Can be classified into 2 Types:

  • Malignant
  • Benign

    • Malignant Breast Conditions:
    1. Invasive Ductal Carcinoma (IDC): - IDC is the most common type of breast cancer, originating in the milk ducts and spreading to surrounding breast tissue. - It often presents as a firm lump or mass in the breast, sometimes accompanied by changes in the skin or nipple.
    2. Invasive Lobular Carcinoma (ILC): - ILC begins in the lobules of the breast and has a tendency to spread to other parts of the body. - It may not always present as a distinct lump, making it challenging to detect through physical examination alone.
    3. Triple-Negative Breast Cancer (TNBC): - TNBC is characterized by the absence of estrogen receptor (ER), progesterone receptor (PR), and human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2) expression. - It tends to be more aggressive and less responsive to hormone-based therapies compared to other types of breast cancer.
    4. HER2-Positive Breast Cancer: - HER2-positive breast cancer is characterized by overexpression of the human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2) protein. - This type of breast cancer tends to grow and spread more quickly than other types but can be targeted with HER2-targeted therapies.
    5. Ductal Carcinoma In Situ (DCIS): - DCIS is a non-invasive form of breast cancer where abnormal cells are found in the lining of the milk ducts but have not spread beyond the ducts into surrounding tissue. - While not life-threatening on its own, DCIS may increase the risk of developing invasive breast cancer if left untreated.
    Benign Breast Conditions:
    1. Fibroadenoma: - Fibroadenomas are non-cancerous breast lumps composed of glandular and fibrous tissue. - They are typically smooth, firm, and mobile, often found in younger women and may change in size over time.
    2. Cysts: - Breast cysts are fluid-filled sacs that can develop in the breast tissue, often causing breast lumps that may be tender or painful. - They are more common in women approaching menopause and may fluctuate in size with the menstrual cycle.
    3. Fibrocystic Changes: - Fibrocystic changes refer to non-cancerous changes in breast tissue characterized by the presence of lumps, cysts, and areas of thickening. - Symptoms may include breast pain, tenderness, and swelling, which can vary throughout the menstrual cycle.
    4. Adenosis: - Adenosis is a benign condition characterized by enlarged lobules in the breast tissue. - It may cause breast lumps or nodules and can sometimes mimic the appearance of more serious conditions on imaging studies.
    5. Fat Necrosis: - Fat necrosis occurs when fatty breast tissue undergoes damage or trauma, leading to the formation of scar tissue and nodules. - It can sometimes mimic the appearance of breast cancer on imaging studies but is non-cancerous in nature.

    Understanding Difference between Malignant and Bengin

    Feature Malignant Tumor Benign Tumor
    Cell Growth Uncontrolled and rapid Slow and localized growth
    Spreading (Metastasis) Can spread to other organs Confined to the original site
    Invasiveness Invades surrounding tissues Doesn't invade surrounding tissues
    Risk of Recurrence Higher risk of returning after removal Lower risk of returning after removal
    Treatment Often requires aggressive treatment (surgery, chemo, radiation) May not require treatment, or minimal treatment (surgery)
    Impact on Health Life-threatening if left untreated Usually not life-threatening
